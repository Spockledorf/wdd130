My Favorite City: Petra, Jordan

One of my favorite cites is Petra, Jordan because there is a beautiful Monastery there. The Monastery, known locally as Ad Deir, is one of the largest and most impressive monuments in the ancient city of Petra in Jordan. Carved directly into a massive sandstone cliff during the 1st century AD by the Nabataean civilization, the structure stands about 45 meters (148 ft) tall and 50 meters (164 ft) wide, making it even larger than Petra's famous Treasury. Despite its name, it was probably not originally a monastery—it was most likely a temple dedicated to the Nabataean king Obodas I, who was later worshiped as a god. Centuries later, when Christians occupied Petra, crosses carved into the interior walls led people to refer to it as a monastery, giving it the name used today. Reaching the Monastery requires climbing about 800 stone steps that wind up the mountainside from Petra's main valley. At the top, visitors are rewarded with an enormous, relatively plain but majestic façade carved into the rock face, framed by the rugged desert mountains. Inside, the chamber is large but simple, emphasizing the dramatic architecture of the exterior. The site sits on a plateau with sweeping views over the surrounding wilderness, making it one of the most breathtaking and peaceful locations in Petra.
