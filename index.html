Idaho, United States

Idaho is a state in the northwestern United States known for its rugged landscapes, outdoor recreation, and agricultural production. Much of the state is mountainous and forested, with large wilderness areas such as the Frank Church-River of No Return Wilderness, one of the largest protected wilderness areas in the contiguous United States. Idaho is especially associated with agriculture, producing a significant share of the nation's potato supply and serving as home to the Idaho Potato Commission. The state capital, Boise, is the largest city and a growing technology and cultural center. Idaho is also known for its rivers and mountains, which support activities such as whitewater rafting, fishing, hiking, and skiing, giving the state a strong reputation for outdoor lifestyles and access to natural wilderness.
